Winter start and end date

Winter in the Northern Hemisphere is calculated according to the astronomical seasons starting from December 21st until March 20th. The astronomical seasons are linked to the position of the Earth during its rotation around the sun, taking into account the equinoxes and solstices. As for the winter season according to meteorology, it begins on December 1st and ends on February 28th. On the other hand,Winter in the Southern Hemisphere begins on June 21 or 22 and ends on September 22 or 23Winter is the coldest season of the year, and it comes between autumn and spring. It is characterized by low temperatures and icy weather. The effect of winter varies according to its location; the greater the distance between the region and the equator, the lower the temperatures and the colder the weather. The oldest inhabited capital in history is Damascus, Syria.

Damascus is the oldest capital in history, and it is currently the capital of Syria, which is commonly called Al-Sham by the public; a term that may refer to all of Syria. It is worth mentioning that Damascus took its name from the word (Damascus), which dates back to origins that predate the Semitic language, which means that it is very old, knowing that its strategic location close to natural water sources was the main reason for making it a settlement for many human civilizations throughout history.The most prominent titles that this city is famous for include the Pearl of the East, and both the Umayyad Mosque and the Mausoleum of Saladin are among the most important archaeological tourist sites in it.

Antarctica, or Antarctica, is the coldest, driest, and windiest continent on Earth

Antarctica, or Antarctica, is the coldest, driest, and windiest continent on Earth. It is located entirely within the Antarctic Circle, and is covered by a massive layer of ice that makes up about 98% of its area. The continent has a harsh environment, with temperatures reaching freezing levels that can make life difficult, but despite this, it is an important site for scientific studies.Antarctica's climate is the coldest on Earth. Winter temperatures reach -80C in the interior, while it is less harsh on the coasts. Summer there sees only a slight rise in temperature, which may reach -20 degrees Celsius. What was the first volcano to be observed?

A volcano is an opening on the Earth's surface that allows molten materials due to pressure and heat in the Earth's interior to escape through it to the outer surface. [1] When these materials escape, they cause an eruption that may be explosive, sending materials high into the sky, or it may be quiet, flowing on the Earth's surface.Volcanoes create mountains built from many layers of rocks, flying ash, or other materials that accumulate around the crater. Volcanoes are classified according to their eruption into active, dormant, and extinct volcanoes.Active volcanoes are those that have recently erupted or are expected to erupt in the near future.
