COVID-19 (Coronavirus disease 2019) is a viral disease.

COVID-19 (coronavirus disease 2019) is a viral illness. The virus that causes it is called severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2. It is more commonly known as SARS-CoV-2. It began spreading in late 2019 and became a pandemic in 2020.The most common way COVID-19 spreads is through the air, where the virus is carried in small respiratory droplets that spread from person to person through close contact. Many people with COVID-19 have no symptoms or only mild symptoms. But for older adults and people with certain medical conditions, COVID-19 can lead to hospitalization or death.

Alzheimer's is the most common cause of dementia, impairing mental and social skills and impairing daily functioning.

Alzheimer's disease is not a natural stage of aging, but the risk of developing it increases with age. About 5% of people aged 65-74 suffer from Alzheimer's disease, while the percentage of people aged 85 and over who suffer from Alzheimer's disease reaches about 50%.Alzheimer's disease may destroy the patient's ability to remember. In the first stage of Alzheimer's, there is a slight loss of memory and states of confusion and disorientation, which ultimately leads to permanent, irreparable damage to the patient's mental abilities, and destroys his ability to remember, think logically, learn and imagine.
