Liverpool star requests move to Real Madrid

English full-back Trent Alexander-Arnold, a Liverpool player, has requested a transfer to Real Madrid as his contract with the English team approaches the end of its term. Arnold enters a free transfer period starting from January 2025, during which any club is allowed to negotiate with him if he does not renew his current contract with Liverpool. According to the Spanish newspaper "Marca", the 26-year-old has decided to play in Spain.The Spanish capital club hopes to combine Arnold and his compatriot Bellingham in the team, especially since the right-back position is only filled by Dani Carvajal, 32, who is currently recovering from a cruciate ligament tear, and his teammate Vazquez, who occupies it in alternative or emergency situations.

La Liga welcomes decision to reject Olmo's registration appeal

La Liga has welcomed a court decision on Friday to reject Barcelona's appeal over Dani Olmo's registration for the remainder of the season, meaning the Spain international will not play for the Catalan club from January 1. Barcelona signed Olmo in August for around €55 million, but due to the club's inability to meet La Liga's salary cap, the attacking midfielder was only registered for the first half of the season.The club succeeded in registering Olmo after long-term injuries kept the first-team players out of the squad for several months, which allowed Barcelona to allocate part of their salaries to register the player (26 years old).

German doping expert calls for Sinner to be punished

Italian tennis star Jannik Sinner, the world number one, should be punished retrospectively for a doping offence, a German anti-doping expert has said, saying it would help fight the use of banned substances. Sinner tested positive twice in March for low levels of clostebol, a substance that can help build muscle mass.Sinner's appeal against his provisional suspension was accepted and he was cleared of error and negligence by the International Tennis Integrity Agency, explaining that the substance entered his system accidentally through an over-the-counter product his physiotherapist used to treat a small cut on his hand.
